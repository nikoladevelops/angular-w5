What is crypto?

Unlike traditional currencies issued by governments (fiat currencies), cryptocurrencies operate on decentralized networks based on blockchain technology.

How to buy crypto?

Cryptocurrency exchanges are platforms where you can buy, sell, and trade cryptocurrencies

Why use crypto?

Decentralization:

Most cryptocurrencies are decentralized and operate on technology called blockchain, which is a distributed ledger maintained by a network of computers (nodes). There is no central authority, like a bank or government, controlling the currency.

Blockchain Technology:

A blockchain is a continuously growing list of records, called blocks, which are linked and secured using cryptographic techniques. Each block contains transaction data, a timestamp, and a reference to the previous block, ensuring transparency and security.

Cryptographic Security:

Cryptocurrencies use cryptographic methods to secure transactions and control the creation of new units. Public-key cryptography allows users to generate a pair of keys: a public key, which serves as an address, and a private key, which is used to sign transactions and prove ownership.

Digital Nature:

Cryptocurrencies exist only in digital form and are not tangible like paper money or coins. They can be stored in digital wallets, which can be software-based or hardware devices.

Limited Supply:

Many cryptocurrencies have a capped supply, meaning there is a maximum limit to how many units can ever be created. For example, Bitcoin has a maximum supply of 21 million coins.
